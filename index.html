VR

Virtual Reality. The most prominent examples being Video-Games/Simulations. The term VR connotates more to mean experiences using a VR headset, versus games and gaming.


AR

Augmented Reality, is the combining/overlaying of virtual reality with the real world.

AR

The Oculus Rift

The Crown Jewel of VR

The Oculus Ducktape

At PAX Prime 2012 an early prototype was shown and it already started to draw attention. (Sample news coverage)

DK1

In 2012, Oculus started a kickstarter to raise $250K to build DIY starter kits for other VR enthusiasts. They ended up with $2.4M at close.

Features/Specs

  • Stereoscopic 3D View
  • Ultra Wide FOV
  • Head Tracking (Orientation only)
  • Software development kit
  • Military grade but already made at sub $400 cost
  • See Product Page for more details

Issues

  • Motion sickness
  • Blurry
  • Screen door effect

Crystal Cove

First demoed at CES 2014, Oculus introduced a new much improved version of the Oculus Rift.

Head Tracking Camera

With a newly added external camera the ability to track accurate head translation as well as orientation was enabled.

DK2

The crystal cove prototype became the basis for the second publicly sold version of the product. It is still marketed to developers to build games ahead of the future release of a consumer version.

New Features/Specs

  • 1000hz sensors
  • 980 x 1080 per eye resolution (Solving the screen door issu)
  • Low persitence screens (Solving much of the juddering, blur issues)
  • Head translation tracking (Done via headset markers and external camera)
  • Software development kit v2
  • Build in latency tester
  • See Product Page for more details

Issues

  • No full body tracking
  • Still need a compelling controller solution
  • Still are totally blind to mischevious friends/sibliings when wearing it.

Future Oculus Versions

  • Pass through video via front camera (Note: Mall practical joke and gillitine)
  • AR possibilities
  • Potentially have hand and peripheral tracking using front facing camera
  • Removing the external tracking camera via Project Tango like tech.
  • Building a CPU/GPU into the headset (Basically a mobile phone)
  • With all of the above, you no have most AR/VR possilities covered including full body movement tracking.

Audio

Game Audio

Advanced game audio engines already model your head to emulates sound wave propagation. This includes head oclusion, ear direction, echo, doppler effect, the geometry of the environment, and more.

VR Audio

With head tracking you can tilt your head to better locate a sound's source. Hear better by leaning out of a door to checkout a commotion in a hallway.

Subtle audio

Imagine leaning in to better hear a buzzing bee on a flower. Flies will now be just as annoying as in real life as they fly into your ear.
